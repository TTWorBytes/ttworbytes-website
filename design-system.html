INTERNAL CREATIVE GUIDE

TTWORBYTES Design Language

Stories first. Design second.

Every page should make visitors feel something before it tells them something.

Every important message deserves room to breathe.

CORE PHILOSOPHY

What Every Page Must Protect

People Before Plots

Visitors should care about the people in a story before they are asked to understand every detail of the plot.

Stories Before Sales

The website should invite exploration rather than behave like a catalogue shouting for attention.

Emotion Before Decoration

Every visual choice must support the emotional purpose of the section.

Curiosity Does the Work

Give visitors enough to care, but leave them with questions that encourage them to keep exploring.

COLOUR SYSTEM

The TTWORBYTES Palette

Dark cinematic foundations, warm gold emphasis and cool blue atmosphere.

Background

#0B1020

The main canvas for the website and the foundation of the star field.

Surface

rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.05)

Used for glass panels and cards that need gentle separation from the background.

Gold

#D6A94A

Reserved for moments of importance, emphasis and invitation.

Atmospheric Blue

#4FA8FF

Used sparingly for light, depth and the feeling of entering another world.

TYPOGRAPHY

Hierarchy Through Type

EYEBROW TEXT

Hero Heading

Section Heading

Character or Card Title

“Important emotional lines deserve room, scale and contrast.”

Body text should remain easy to read, calm in tone and comfortably spaced.

NEGATIVE SPACE

Important Messages Need Silence Around Them

Large moments deserve generous space above and below.

Never fill a gap simply because it exists.

Space gives visitors time to absorb the previous idea before meeting the next one.

CHARACTER INTRODUCTIONS

Introduce People as People

Character sections should create affection, recognition and curiosity—not read like encyclopaedia entries.

Portrait

Character Name

“A memorable line that captures the person's spirit.”

Explain why readers would enjoy spending time with this person.

Alternate portrait placement from left to right to create rhythm.

The central character may receive more scale or space, but every important person deserves dignity and presence.

GLASS PANELS

Frame Ideas, Not Everything

Use For

Story introductions, belief statements, closing messages and important explanatory sections.

Avoid For

Character portraits, every heading, every image or any element that already has enough visual presence.

BUTTON LANGUAGE

Every Button Should Make an Honest Promise

Homepage Explore Books
Series Page Enter Story
Book Opening Learn More
Page Ending Back to Series

THE TTWORBYTES PRINCIPLES

The Rules That Do Not Change

1. People before plots.

2. Stories before sales.

3. Every important message deserves room to breathe.

4. Introduce people as friends, not biographies.

5. Let curiosity do the work.

6. Design should support emotion, never compete with it.

TTWORBYTES exists to create stories that remind us that ordinary people can live extraordinary days.

Whether the journey takes place in a classroom, on another planet or somewhere in between, every story begins with people who care about each other.

If readers finish one of our stories smiling, thinking about someone they love, or believing they can be a little braver tomorrow than they were today, then we've succeeded.
