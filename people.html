THE CENTRAL CHARACTER

Jake Morrow

“If worrying was an Olympic sport, Jake would win gold.”

Jake is twelve, easily embarrassed and capable of imagining seventeen disasters before breakfast. Beneath the panic is a fiercely loyal boy who would do anything for the people he loves.

First appearance: This Is My Last Day

Appears in this story

JAKE'S TWIN BROTHER

Jack Morrow

“Jack has a way of making difficult days feel smaller.”

Jack is thoughtful, quietly funny and often the first person to notice when something is troubling Jake. Their relationship lies at the emotional heart of the series.

First appearance: This Is My Last Day

Appears in this story

WRESTLER, FRIEND, FORCE OF NATURE

Ruby Mason

“She looks like she could fold me into a thousand pieces.”

Ruby is confident, capable and never lets fear decide what she can do. She values kindness far more than confidence or popularity—and Jake notices everything about her.

First appearance: This Is My Last Day

Appears in this story

JAKE'S BEST FRIEND

Alex

“If Alex says, ‘I've got an idea,’ Jake starts worrying.”

Alex is funny, optimistic and remarkably skilled at turning ordinary moments into unforgettable trouble. Behind the jokes is a loyal friend who always stands beside Jake.

First appearance: This Is My Last Day

PHYSICAL EDUCATION TEACHER

Mr Briggs

"Every challenge is easier once you've stopped making excuses."

Mr Briggs may look intimidating, but he cares deeply about every pupil in his class. He believes confidence is built through effort, resilience and never giving up, and somehow always knows when someone needs a quiet word of encouragement more than another lap of the field.

MATHS TEACHER

Mr Thornton

"Some teachers teach maths. Mr Thornton teaches people how to think."

Patient, thoughtful and quietly brilliant, Mr Thornton has a remarkable gift for making even the most difficult problems seem achievable. He doesn't simply teach mathematics—he teaches his pupils how to think, encouraging them to find answers for themselves.

First appearance: This Is My Last Day

SCHOOL BULLY

Bradley Cooper

"Bradley Cooper has never confused being feared with being respected."

Bradley Cooper enjoys being the toughest boy in school and rarely misses an opportunity to remind everyone else of it. Used to getting his own way through intimidation, he soon discovers that not everyone is prepared to back down from a bully.

First appearance: This Is My Last Day

SCHOOLMATE

Tim Dawson

"Tim believes every day is going to be a good one... and somehow makes everyone else believe it too."

Although he's a year younger than Jake, Tim's cheerful optimism makes him impossible not to like. Friendly, curious and always eager to help, he has a wonderful habit of bringing out the best in the people around him, often without even realising it.

First appearance: This Is My Big Mistake

New Classmate

Jake & Jack's Mum

"Whatever happens, we'll get through it together."

Jake and Jack's mum. Kind-hearted, practical and quietly determined, Mrs Morrow has an infectious smile that can brighten even the worst of days. Whether she's baking pies, organising family holidays or keeping everyone together when life becomes difficult, she is the steady heart of the Morrow family.

First appearance: This Is My Big Mistake

Jake & Jack's Dad

"Come on then... let's see where this clue takes us."

Jake and Jack's dad. A skilled offshore worker on the North Sea oil rigs, Mr Morrow spends weeks away from home before returning to the family he loves. Reliable, patient and quietly humorous, he treasures every moment he can spend with his wife and sons.
